# Arabic A2 - Everyday Fusha Conversations

## Level Outcome

Learners can handle short everyday Fusha conversations, ask simple follow-up
questions, make plans, describe simple past experiences, ask for service help,
and give short reasons while maintaining clear formal Arabic pronunciation and
basic reading support.

## Scope

- Formal Arabic / Modern Standard Arabic, not dialect.
- Indonesian explanations for adult learners.
- Arabic script with harakat for learner-facing examples and listening scripts.
- Conversation-first lessons with reading and writing support.
- Short everyday domains: social contact, plans, transport, services, health,
  recent past, opinions, and review conversations.
- No religious source text or devotional TTS content.

## A2 Upgrade From A1

- Dialogue length: 6-10 turns.
- Learner responses may combine two short clauses.
- Introduce simple reasons with `لِأَنَّ`.
- Introduce near future with `سَـ` and simple intention with `أُرِيدُ أَنْ`.
- Introduce simple past with common verbs such as `ذَهَبْتُ`, `دَرَسْتُ`,
  `اشْتَرَيْتُ`, and `رَأَيْتُ`.
- Introduce polite arrangements: inviting, accepting, declining, rescheduling,
  confirming time/place.
- Keep vocabulary concrete and everyday.

## Passing Threshold

- Overall score: 72
- Speaking / Conversation: 65
- Listening: 65
- Pronunciation: 60
- Vocabulary / Useful Phrases: 65
- Grammar: 65
- Reading: 58
- Writing: 58
- Lesson completion: 80%

Speaking and listening are critical skills. The learner cannot pass Arabic A2
if either is below its minimum score.
