# Arabic A1 - Formal Foundations

## Level Outcome

Learners can recognize and produce very simple Arabic phrases for greetings, introductions, classroom instructions, and asking for clarification, while beginning to understand short formal study-circle expressions.

## Scope

- Formal Arabic / Modern Standard Arabic, not amiyah.
- Indonesian explanations for adult learners.
- Arabic script first, with transliteration support where useful.
- Practical conversation plus early exposure to formal classroom and reading language.

## Skill Weights

| Skill | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Speaking / Conversation Response | 30% |
| Listening Comprehension | 25% |
| Pronunciation & Fluency | 15% |
| Vocabulary / Useful Phrases | 10% |
| Grammar Accuracy | 10% |
| Reading Comprehension | 5% |
| Writing | 5% |

## Passing Threshold

- Overall score: 70
- Speaking / Conversation: 60
- Listening: 60
- Pronunciation: 55
- Vocabulary / Useful Phrases: 60
- Grammar: 60
- Reading: 55
- Writing: 55
- Lesson completion: 80%

Speaking and listening are critical skills. The learner cannot pass Arabic A1 if either is below its minimum score.

## Quality Reference

- Keep Arabic formal and beginner-safe.
- Use harakat in learner-facing Arabic examples and listening scripts.
- Keep every lesson conversation-first: recognize the phrase, hear it in context, say it aloud, respond with it, and use it in guided roleplay.
